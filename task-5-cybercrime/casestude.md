# Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness Case Study

## 🎣 Phishing Case Study: The "Urgent Account Verification" Scam

### Overview
Phishing is a deceptive cybercrime where attackers masquerade as a trusted entity—such as a bank, a popular social media platform, or a university department—to steal sensitive data like login credentials and credit card numbers.

This crime typically targets anyone with an active email or messaging account.

### Step-by-Step Scenario
1. **The Initial Contact**: The victim receives an "urgent" email appearing to be from their university’s IT department or a professional service like LinkedIn.
2. **The Deception**: The message claims there is a "security breach" or an "unauthorized login attempt" and instructs the user to click a link immediately to "verify their identity" and avoid account suspension.
3. **The Trap**: This link leads to a fake, look-alike website designed to capture the user's username and password.
4. **The Breach**: Once the victim enters their credentials on the phishing site, the attacker gains full access to their real account.

### Impact and Consequences
From the compromised account, the criminal can steal personal documents, change recovery settings, or use the account to spread further phishing links to the victim's entire contact list. College students are frequently targeted due to their heavy reliance on digital platforms for academics and networking. The consequences include identity theft, loss of access to critical academic accounts, and further criminal activity committed using the victim's identity.
